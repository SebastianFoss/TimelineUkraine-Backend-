    Our Mission

      Finding accurate, relevant, and "straight to the point" news on the internet is often times difficult, due to source bias, misinformation, hidden agendas, or sometimes simply a lack of information as a whole because the topic no longer appeals to the algorithm. TimelineUkraine is an attempt to combat this by relying on only trusted sources, student volunteer writers, and focusing only on events in Ukraine, even when in disregard to shifts in popular attention (which in recent times have proven to come quickly). TimelineUkraine is still in development, and there will be many features to come, so please send any feedback you would like to provide. Special thanks to all current and future writers for providing news! And a special thanks to you, for choosing TimelineUkraine!

  • How are sources chosen?

      We strive to find the most reliable sources possible, to ensure timely and unbiased reporting of real news from events worldwide surrounding the invasion of Ukraine. Writers independently choose sources that have proven to be as unbiased and truthful as possible.

The Cybersecurity Specialist

Microsoft recently seized seven domains linked to Russian hackers. According to Microsoft, the sites were operated by Strontium, a group with close ties to the Russian government. The sites were used to target Ukrainian media outlets and government agencies in the US and EU. Siezed servers are now being redirected to a sinkhole, where cybersecurity experts can analyze malicious connections. Furthermore, security vendor WatchGuard fixed a vulnerability in its firewall that was exploited by Russian hackers. The flaw was used by Russian spies to create a bot net using Cyclops Blink malware. After the exploit was reported by the FBI, security analysts blasted WatchGuard for their attempts to cover up the extent of damages. The company waited 8 months to release an official public vulnerability announcement, and company representatives have refused to comment on the inexplicable and dangerous delay.



Source: The Verge, Ars Technica

Global food shortage

Due to war sanctions, global food shortages might be a coming problem. Ukraine and Russia produce around 14% of the world's wheat and accounts for around 28% of the wheat global market. Currently, 13.5 million tons of wheat and 16 million tons of corn are stuck in Russia and Ukraine because of these sanctions. Ukraine and Russia also export around 28% of fertilizers made from phosphorus, nitrogen, and potassium which would worsen the global food situation. Around 2 weeks ago President Biden warns of “real” food shortages and Vladimir Putin warns western nations how sanctions will cause food shortages in the “poorest regions of the world”.

Sunday, 3/20/2022
-Russia’s Assault on Mariupol-

As the Russian military presses closer into the heart of Ukraine, another city is taking the brunt of Putin’s attacks, that of Mariupol. Mariupol is a port city in southeastern Ukraine with over 400,000 citizens that have been at the center of an ongoing humanitarian crisis. It has been the site of a bombed theatre, hospital, and other civilian buildings which have caused over 2300 citizen casualties in the city over the course of the past three weeks alone; where many of the deceased are unable to be buried due to its dangers. The Mariupol city government has also made claims that its citizens were being forcibly deported to Russia through a sports club this weekend. Many citizens are also unable to flee the war-torn zone as railways and roads throughout the region have been severely damaged, and as Russia continues to siege the port city, necessities such as food, water, and good electricity are scarce. In a shocking move, Putin has recently delivered a 5am Moscow time deadline (March 21st, 2022) for surrender from the city’s government, asking to have citizens and troops stop fighting. When this happens, Colonel-General Mikhail Mizintsev (director of the Russian National Defense Management Center) claims that everyone in the port city will be able to escape and humanitarian corridors will be open to them starting at 10am the next day. What is left to see is Ukraine’s response to the situation.

Sources: The Guardian, NPR, The Hill

Thursday, 3/17/22
In a Monumental Effort, Ukraine Connects with the European Power Grid

Since Russia’s 2014 annexation of the former Ukrainian peninsula Crimea, Ukraine has made efforts to join with other European countries in a connected power grid, and has done so today in a monumental milestone linking the country to one of the largest continental European power grids (ENTSO-E). By doing so, Ukraine has been released from its dependence on Russia's Integrated Power System and allows Ukraine to continue to receive power if Russia continues to destroy Ukrainian infrastructure.


*ENTSO-E stands for the European association for the cooperation of transmission system operators (TSOs)
